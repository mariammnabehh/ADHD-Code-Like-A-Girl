ADHD Types

Predominantly Inattentive

Individuals with predominantly inattentive presentation of ADHD primarily struggle with attention-related symptoms. They may have difficulty sustaining attention, following instructions, organizing tasks, and completing assignments or chores. They may appear forgetful, easily distracted, and frequently lose or misplace items.

Predominantly Hyperactive

individuals exhibit primarily hyperactive and impulsive symptoms. They may be excessively restless, fidgety, and have difficulty remaining seated for extended periods. They often talk excessively, interrupt others, and have trouble waiting their turn. While attention difficulties may still be present, they are typically overshadowed by hyperactivity and impulsivity.

Combined

The combined presentation is the most common subtype of ADHD. Individuals with this presentation experience a combination of both inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive symptoms. They may have difficulty focusing and maintaining attention, as well as exhibiting hyperactive and impulsive behaviors. This subtype often presents the most significant challenges across various settings, including school, work.

How To Deal With ADHD

  • Education and Understanding: Learn as much as you can about ADHD, including its symptoms, causes, and treatment options. Understanding the condition can empower you to effectively manage its challenges.

  • Professional Evaluation and Treatment: Seek a comprehensive evaluation from a qualified healthcare professional, such as a psychiatrist or psychologist, for an accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment plan. Treatment may include medication, therapy, or a combination of both.

  • Healthy Lifestyle Habits: Healthy Lifestyle Habits: Maintain a balanced diet, regular exercise, and adequate sleep. These lifestyle factors can help improve focus, attention, and overall well-being.

  • Flexibility and Self-Compassion: Flexibility and Self-Compassion: Be patient and kind to yourself. Accept that managing ADHD may involve setbacks and challenges along the way. Focus on progress rather than perfection, and celebrate your successes, no matter how small.

  • Social Support: Seek support from friends, family members, support groups, or online communities. Connecting with others who understand your experiences can provide validation, encouragement, and practical advice.
